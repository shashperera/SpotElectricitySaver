💡 Electricity Saving Tips

How to cut your electricity bill in Finland with a spot price (pörssisähkö) contract.

⚡ How Finnish Spot Pricing Works – Daily Pattern
🌅
Morning Peak (06–09)
Everyone wakes up at once. Prices spike. Avoid washing machine, sauna, and dishwasher during this window.
☀️
Daytime (09–16)
Solar production is higher. Prices often moderate. Good time to run dishwasher or washing machine if you're home.
🌆
Evening Peak (17–21)
People come home, cook, use sauna. Most expensive window. Avoid heavy appliances — TV or kettle only.
🌙
Night (22–06)
Lowest demand = cheapest prices. Set timers on dishwasher, washing machine, and robot vacuum for this window!
🏆 Top 8 Tips for Kontula
1
Set a timer on your washing machine — start it at 01:00–04:00 when prices are lowest. Most machines have a delay-start feature.
2
Avoid the sauna during 17:00–21:00 — peak pricing can make it 3–5× more expensive than heating at 23:00.
3
Only boil what you need — a full 1.7L kettle uses ~0.15 kWh. Half-fill it and save 50% every time.
4
Check tomorrow's prices at 14:00 — Nord Pool publishes next-day prices daily. Plan your heavy usage a day ahead.
5
Run the dishwasher at night — use delay start and run at 01:00–03:00 for maximum savings without interrupting your day.
6
Charge your vacuum at night — robot and cordless vacuums should charge during the cheapest hours (00:00–06:00).
7
Air fryer vs oven — an air fryer uses about half the energy of a traditional oven. Use it during cheap hours instead of a big oven.
8
Spot vs fixed contract — with a spot (pörssisähkö) contract, shifting usage to cheap hours can save €10–30/month depending on your habits!

📋 What Does the Spot Price Include?
📊
Spot Price (shown in this app)
Variable, hourly. Typically 2–20 c/kWh in Finland. Includes 24% VAT.
🔌
Transfer Fee (siirtohinnoittelu)
Fixed ~3–5 c/kWh. Paid to your local grid operator (e.g., Helen, Caruna). Not shown here.
🏛️
Electricity Tax
Fixed ~2.7 c/kWh set by Finnish government. Added on top of the spot price. Not shown here.
🏢
Supplier Margin
~0.2–0.5 c/kWh added by your electricity company (e.g., Helen, Fortum). Not shown here.
